Gansu Province confirms 2 confirmed cases of pneumonia due to novel coronavirus infection

Release time: 2020-01-27 21:38:05 Traffic:
On January 23, according to the National Health and Health Commission's latest diagnosis of pneumonia of the first new type of coronavirus infection in each province and the "Diagnosis and Treatment Program of New Coronavirus Infected Pneumonia (Trial Version 3)", Gansu Province confirmed 2 new types of coronavirus. Confirmed cases of viral infection of pneumonia.
Wu Moumou, the first suspected case in Gansu Province, was confirmed to be a new type of coronavirus nucleic acid by reviewing the laboratory of the Chinese Center for Disease Control and Prevention, and combined with the clinical manifestations and epidemiological history of the patient, it was confirmed as a confirmed case of pneumonia of new coronavirus infection.
At the same time, a suspected case was found in Baiyin City, Gansu Province. The patient was a 24-year-old male who worked in Wuhan, Hubei Province. He had a history of activities in the Wuhan Farmers Market within 14 days before the onset of illness and was clearly exposed to patients with fever and respiratory symptoms. He developed fever, fatigue, cough, headache, and muscle soreness on January 16 and left Wuhan on January 19 and arrived at his home in Huining County on the 20th. At 12:00 on January 22, he went to the designated hospital for treatment by himself, that is, he was admitted to the hospital for isolation and treatment. He is currently in a stable condition. Tested by the provincial and Baiyin CDC laboratories, they were all positive for new-type coronavirus nucleic acid. Based on the clinical manifestations and epidemiological history of the patient, they were confirmed by the provincial medical treatment expert group for consultation and confirmed as confirmed cases of pneumonitis with new-type coronavirus infection. .
After an epidemiological investigation of 2 confirmed cases, a total of 41 close contacts were investigated, all of which were in home isolation and medical observation.
