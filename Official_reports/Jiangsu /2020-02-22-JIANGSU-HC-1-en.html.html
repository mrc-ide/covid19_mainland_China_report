begin->No new confirmed cases of new coronavirus pneumonia in Jiangsu on February 21 end->
Release date: begin->2020-02-22 08:46:42 end-> Views:
begin->

At 04:00 on February 21, 2020, there were no new confirmed cases of new coronavirus pneumonia in Jiangsu Province. 35 new cases were discharged. 1 new critical case (previously severe case).

As of 24:00 on February 21, Jiangsu Province had reported a total of 631 confirmed cases of new coronavirus pneumonia. Among them, 49 were light cases, 572 were ordinary cases, 5 were severe cases, and 5 were critical cases. There were 387 discharged patients. Among the confirmed cases, there were 93 cases in Nanjing, 55 in Wuxi, 79 in Xuzhou, 51 in Changzhou, 87 in Suzhou, 40 in Nantong, 48 in Lianyungang, 66 in Huai'an, 27 in Yancheng, and Yangzhou. There were 23 cases in city, 12 cases in Zhenjiang city, 37 cases in Taizhou city, and 13 cases in Suqian city. Among the severe cases, one was in Nanjing, one in Xuzhou, one in Suzhou, one in Huai'an, and one in Zhenjiang. Among the severe cases, one was in Nanjing, one in Wuxi, one in Lianyungang, one in Huai'an, and one in Yancheng. Among the discharged patients, 52 were from Nanjing, 37 from Wuxi, 58 from Xuzhou, 36 from Changzhou, 42 from Suzhou, 28 from Nantong, 22 from Lianyungang, 35 from Huaian, 20 from Yancheng, and Yangzhou. There were 15 cases in Beijing, 9 cases in Zhenjiang, 25 cases in Taizhou, and 8 cases in Suqian.

At present, Jiangsu Province has tracked 12,592 close contacts, 11,466 medical observations have been lifted, and 1126 are still receiving medical observations.

Experts suggest that the public's personal protection precautions in the near future: maintain hand hygiene, and wash your hands properly before and after meals and after contact with easily contaminated items. Fruits and vegetables should be cleaned before consumption. Vegetables and meat should be cooked thoroughly before eating. Fruits should be peeled. Cutting boards and knives for raw and cooked food should be separated and hands should be washed in time. Try to eat less cold salads, not raw water, overnight water, or long-term barrel water.

Unit protection precautions: Actively carry out patriotic health campaigns. All units should do a good job of environmental hygiene, and strengthen daily cleaning and disinfection of toilets and toilets. Units should minimize collective activities. Canteens should strengthen the disinfection of tableware and utensils, strengthen indoor ventilation and ventilation, and improve indoor air quality. Separate dishes from raw and cooked, keep containers clean and dry. Employees are encouraged to dine at the wrong peak during meals, avoid meals as much as possible, and keep a certain distance when lining up and eating.
