Epidemic situation of new coronavirus pneumonia in Tibet Autonomous Region on February 12, 2020

Author: Editor: Translation: Share to:

As of 24:00 on February 12, there were 0 confirmed cases, 0 severe cases, 0 death cases, 0 suspected cases, and 1 cured case in our district. A total of one pneumonia case with a new coronavirus infection was confirmed.

 

Tips from the Tibet Autonomous Region Health Committee: Protective suggestions on returning to work

1. Take your own body temperature every day, if your body temperature rises or if you have respiratory symptoms, it is recommended to take the initiative to apply for rest or work at home

2. Always wear a mask and carry disinfectant, paper towels and other items when going out. Whenever possible, choose to walk, cycle, drive or commute to and from work. When using public transport, try to keep a distance from other people, avoid talking, and avoid touching the objects and others on the car;

3. Try to avoid taking the box elevator. It is recommended to walk up and down stairs without touching the handrail. If you are using a box elevator, you must also wear a mask. You can directly isolate the elevator buttons without using your hands. You can use paper towels and other objects to isolate them.

4. Before entering the workplace, cooperate with the unit to do a temperature check and health inquiry as required;

5. Do a good job in daily disinfection and cleaning of workplaces, offices, bathrooms (door handles, desktops, floors, etc.) and office supplies (computer keyboards, mice, public phones, mobile phones, stationery, etc.), and keep the room regularly ventilated, avoid using the central air conditioning;

6. When working in a multi-person office, you should try to sit as far apart as possible, wear a mask all the time, keep a moderate distance for conversation, and reduce face-to-face communication. Minimize centralized meetings as much as possible. It is recommended to innovate office methods and use video conferencing and online communication. If the meeting must be centralized, the length of the meeting must be controlled, and a mask must be worn throughout the process;

7. To maintain good sanitary habits, try to avoid the crowd when coughing or sneezing, cover your nose and mouth with paper towels, handkerchiefs or elbows to prevent saliva splashing. Put the used paper towels in the trash, and wash your hands immediately after touching the respiratory secretions;

8. Wash your hands after receiving items such as courier and documents. Develop the habit of carrying a signature pen with you, and avoid using public pens as much as possible;

9. Try to bring your own meals at work. If you go to the cafeteria or restaurant for dinner, it is recommended to stagger the peaks and avoid grouping; try to sit separately when you eat to reduce the chance of face-to-face dining; do not speak when you eat;

10. Do not stay in the unit after work, do not party or dinner after work;

11. Before going home, put your coat, shoes, hat, backpack, mobile phone, glasses, etc. in a fixed position and disinfect and clean them in time, then wash your hands and face, and open the window for ventilation;

12. Try not to go out after going home. You can exercise at home moderately, drink plenty of water, pay attention to a healthy and balanced diet, and pay attention to rest.
