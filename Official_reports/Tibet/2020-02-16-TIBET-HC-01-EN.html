Epidemic situation of new coronavirus pneumonia in Tibet Autonomous Region on February 15, 2020

Author: edit: translation: share to:

 

As of 24:00 on February 15, there were 0 confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia, 0 severe cases, 0 death cases, 0 suspected cases, and 1 discharged case in our district. A total of 1 case of new coronary pneumonia was confirmed.

 

Tips from the Tibet Autonomous Region Health and Health Committee: Self-health monitoring and management of provisional guidelines for pregnant women to prevent new coronary pneumonia

1.Do a good job of self-health monitoring. Pay attention to daily temperature and weight changes, check for symptoms of respiratory infections, and monitor fetal movements regularly.

2.In the first trimester, if the B-ultrasound has confirmed the intrauterine pregnancy, and there is a slight abdominal pain or a small amount of bleeding, you can rest at home for observation; if you repeatedly have a small amount of irregular bleeding, you should promptly consult an appointment with an obstetrician and gynecologist to perform a B-ultrasound to rule out fetal Stop childbirth; if abdominal pain worsens or bleeding volume increases, or intrauterine pregnancy has not been confirmed by B-ultrasound, you should promptly consult a gynecologist and consult a doctor.

3.During the high epidemic period, if there is no special situation, pregnant women can consult the obstetrician to appropriately postpone the check-up time and monitor the fetal movement (fetal movement) at home. When a birth checkup is necessary, make an appointment in advance to take care of it and minimize the time to seek medical treatment. Pregnant women with complications of pregnancy and complications should be treated strictly according to doctor's advice. If you have abnormalities during pregnancy (headache, unclear vision, palpitation, shortness of breath, increased blood pressure, vaginal bleeding or fluid, abnormal abdominal pain, abnormal fetal movement, etc.) or signs of childbirth, you should seek medical treatment in time. Don't delay medical treatment because of fear or worry.

4.When the pregnant woman has mild symptoms such as nasal congestion and throat discomfort, if there is no history of travel, residence or close contact with patients with coronavirus infection in the epidemic area within 14 days, no fever, you can observe at home, take sufficient rest, monitor body temperature daily and observe Symptoms change.

5.If the maternal has a history of travel or residence in the epidemic-prone area within 14 days, or has a history of close contact with patients diagnosed with coronavirus infection, medical observation is required at home or at a designated place. During the observation period, pregnant women should pay close attention to their symptoms and monitor fetal movements. . If you have suspicious symptoms (including fever, cough, sore throat, chest tightness, dyspnea, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, conjunctivitis, muscle soreness, etc.), don't panic, contact the community manager or medical observer immediately to inform yourself accurately Get medical attention in a timely manner.

6.Breastfeeding is not recommended until maternal isolation is lifted. Newborns are advised to be isolated for 10-14 days, and the status of the newborns should be closely monitored. If any discomfort occurs during this period, you should seek medical treatment in a timely manner.
