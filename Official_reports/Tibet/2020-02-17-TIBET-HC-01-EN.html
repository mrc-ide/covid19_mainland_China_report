Epidemic Situation of New Coronavirus Pneumonia in Tibet Autonomous Region on February 16, 2020

Author: edit: translation: share to:

 

As of 24:00 on February 16, there were 0 confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia in our district, 0 severe cases, 0 death cases, 0 suspected cases, and 1 discharged case. A total of 1 case of new coronary pneumonia was confirmed.

 

Tips of the Tibet Autonomous Region Health and Health Committee

1.If you are not going to the hospital for medical checkups, you should go to the nearest medical institution that can meet the needs and have a small number of outpatients; give priority to the necessary and urgently needed medical examinations and medical operations; make appointments and preparations before the consultation, and be familiar with the layout and steps of the hospital. May reduce consultation time. For the check-up, you should choose a hospital with a file and pay attention to protection.

2.On the way to the hospital and in the hospital, pay attention to cold protection and avoid colds; pregnant women and accompanying family members should wear medical surgical masks throughout the process; they can carry hand-free lotion or disinfectant wipes to maintain hand hygiene; while on the road and in the hospital, Keep the distance between people as much as possible (at least 1 meter).

3.Avoid public transportation when going out for medical treatment. You can choose to take a taxi or self-driving car. If necessary, open the windows to facilitate air circulation in the car.

4.After contacting hospital items such as hospital door handles, curtains, and doctor's white coats, try to use hand disinfectant as much as possible. If you cannot remove them in time, do not touch the mouth, nose, or eyes.

5.After going home, you should properly dispose of masks, change clothes, wash your hands, and wash exposed areas such as the face and facial features. Clothing should be washed and disinfected as soon as possible, and the jacket should be placed in a place with good ventilation.
