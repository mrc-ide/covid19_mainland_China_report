Situation of new crown pneumonia in Chongqing as of 20:00 on February 10, 2020
Release time: 2020-02-11 Source: Publicity United Front Office Views: Editors: Office Review: Publicity United Front Office
Font size: small medium large 🖨

At 12:00 to 24:00 on February 10, 2020, Chongqing reported 13 new confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia, 3 new cases of severe cases, 1 case of severe cases converted to common cases, and 6 cases of discharged patients.

Among the newly confirmed cases, 1 in Jiangbei District, 1 in Nan'an District, 2 in Yubei District, 1 in Changshou District, 1 in Dazu District, 1 in Nanan District, 1 in Fengdu County, 1 in Zhong County, Yun 1 case in Yang County, 1 case in Wushan County and 2 cases in Shizhu County; among the newly added heavy cases, 2 were in Wanzhou District and 1 in Jiangbei District; 1 case of heavy cases turned into ordinary cases was Changshou District; among the newly discharged cases, Qianjiang There were 2 cases in District, 1 in Hechuan District, 1 in Dazu District, and 2 in Zhongxian County.

As of 20:00 on February 10, Chongqing has reported a total of 486 confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia; 36 cases of severe cases, 22 cases of severe cases, 2 deaths, and 66 discharged patients. A total of 16,532 close contacts have been tracked, 9,650 have been released from medical observation, and 6,882 people are still receiving medical observation. Details are as follows:

Experts believe that 68.35% of newly confirmed cases came from close contacts recently. Second- and third-generation infections among close contacts are the main source of newly confirmed cases, accounting for 63.64% on February 6, 66.67% on February 7, 65.00% on February 8, and 77.27% on February 9. In this regard, the continuous strengthening of close contact tracking and isolation management in all districts and counties is the key to the current epidemic prevention and control.

The Chongqing Municipal Health and Health Committee has suggested that strengthening the management of close contacts, effectively controlling potential sources of infection, and preventing the spread of the epidemic. The close observation period for close contacts is 14 days after the last contact with the case or infected person. No abnormalities are found after 14 days, and medical observation can be lifted. For close contacts of suspected cases, after the suspected cases are ruled out, close contacts will also be released from medical observation. All persons who are judged to be close contacts are requested to take the initiative to cooperate with medical and health workers to conduct medical observations. This is not only responsible for the health of themselves, their families and friends, but also for the health of the general public.
