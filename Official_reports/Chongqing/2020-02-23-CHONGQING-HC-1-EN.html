Epidemic Situation of New Crown Pneumonia in Chongqing on February 23, 2020
Release time: 2020-02-23 Source: United Front Office Page views:Times Edit: Office Review: United Front Office
font size: small in Big ðŸ–¨

At 04:00 on February 22, 2020, Chongqing reported 1 new confirmed case of new coronary pneumonia, and 12 new cases were cured and discharged.

The newly confirmed cases were 1 in Zhongxian; among the newly cured cases, 1 was in Wanzhou District, 1 in Yuzhong District, 1 in Dadukou District, 1 in Banan District, 3 in Hechuan District, 1 in Luan District, One case was in Dianjiang County and three cases were in Liangjiang New District.

As of 24:00 on February 22, Chongqing currently has 239 confirmed cases in hospital (including 24 severe cases and 10 critical cases), a total of 6 deaths, a total of 328 discharged patients cured, and a cumulative report of 573 example. A total of 23,063 close contacts have been tracked, 21,854 medical observations have been lifted, and 1,209 people are still receiving medical observations. Details are as follows:

Experts believe that through the full coverage of nucleic acid testing in the "four types of people", asymptomatic infections have been found intensively. Early detection of asymptomatic infections reduces community exposure and reduces the risk of community infection. However, some patients with asymptomatic infection will continue to be diagnosed for a period of time. The epidemic situation in our city is still severe. We must implement prevention and control measures based on zoning and classification.

The Chongqing Municipal Health and Health Committee reminds the public to earnestly strengthen their self-protection and minimize activities in crowded public places. If you are going to a place with relatively dense crowds and poor air mobility (such as subways, supermarkets, etc.), you must wear a mask to minimize contact with public goods. After returning from public places, wash your hands immediately. When you are not sure whether your hands are clean, avoid touching your mouth, nose, and eyes.
