Epidemic situation of new coronavirus pneumonia in Guangxi on February 14, 2020

/
Date: 2020-02-15 08:00:06 Source: Autonomous Region Health Committee
/ /
/
/
/ /
/
/
/
/
/ /
/
/
/

 

0-24 February 14, the district added a total of novel coronavirus pneumonia confirmed cases in 9 cases, of which two cases Nanning, Liuzhou two cases, Beihai three cases, one case of Yulin City, Hechi City, one case; new 44 suspected cases were added ; 5 new cases were cured and discharged (1 in Nanning, 3 in Beihai, and 1 in Qinzhou); there were no new deaths.

A total of 235 confirmed cases and 40 discharged patients have been reported in the district. There are 181 suspected cases, 6 severe cases (2 in Nanning, 1 in Guigang, 1 in Yulin, 2 in Hechi), and 6 critical cases (1 in Nanning, 2 in Guilin, and Fangchenggang). 2 cases, 1 case in Hechi City) and 2 deaths (1 case in Hechi City, 1 case in Beihai City).

Of the total confirmed cases , 49 were in Nanning, 24 in Liuzhou, 31 in Guilin, 5 in Wuzhou, 43 in Beihai, 17 in Fangchenggang, 8 in Qinzhou, 8 in Guigang, 10 in Yulin, There were 3 cases in Baise City, 4 cases in Hezhou City, 22 cases in Hechi City, and 11 cases in Laibin City .

At present, a total of 14,280 close contacts have been tracked and 2,397 people are undergoing medical observation.

In order to block the long-distance passenger transport (including high-speed rail, trains, cars) to spread the new crown pneumonia epidemic channel. Experts recommend that we focus on the "four" key links. The first is protection of employees. Practitioners such as passengers, vehicles and station equipment, line operation maintenance, security, and service shop stalls in stations should be included in the employee's health monitoring management, wear masks, and receive body temperature monitoring; rationally arrange employee hours and implement a shift off system. The second is preventive control measures. Install a temperature screening device at the station yard to monitor the temperature of incoming passengers, find fever passengers, guide them to a relatively independent area, and inquire about travel and diagnosis and medical history; find that they have travel history in Hubei and Wuhan or history of passing through key epidemic areas within 14 days and If the family has one or more cases of pneumonia, immediately call 120 to inform the hospital to dispatch a car for treatment; use high-speed railway station billboards, subway TV and other media to post prevention and control information. The third is to carry out facility disinfection and environmental cleaning. Daily cleaning and disinfection of public places (areas) such as steering wheels, carriages, waiting platforms, chairs, ticket offices, toilets, escalator handrails, elevator buttons, trash cans, etc. The toilets are equipped with hand sanitizer to ensure that the faucet is running and washing hands. Rodent. The fourth is to strengthen the ventilation of stations, trains and tunnels. Cleaning, disinfection or replacement of open cooling towers, purifiers, air vents, air handling units, heaters (humidifiers) and other equipment every week to ensure the normal operation of the centralized air conditioning and ventilation system; a new air operation method is used to close the return air Otherwise, the air purification and disinfection device will be activated; the exhaust air from the centralized air conditioning and ventilation system will be discharged directly to the outdoors before the stations are put into operation and 30-60 minutes after the operation is stopped.

Common disinfection methods are: 1. Daily disinfection. Use chlorine disinfectant once a day to wipe and disinfect public places (areas). 2. Disinfect at any time. When a suspected case of new coronary pneumonia is found during operation, contact the local CDC and implement disinfection under its guidance. 3. Terminal disinfection. After the long-distance transportation vehicle reached the end, the CDC disinfected the compartment where the patient was seated in accordance with the requirements of the General Principles of Disinfection of the Epidemic Area (GB 19193-2015).
