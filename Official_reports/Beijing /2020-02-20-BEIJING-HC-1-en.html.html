February 20th Beijing New Crown Pneumonia Prevention and Control Work Press Conference
Date: 2020-02-20                 Source: Beijing Municipal Health Committee

In Beijing, there were 8 new cases of neo-pneumococcal pneumonia. After careful treatment by medical staff, they met discharge standards and were discharged from Ditan Hospital, Fengtai Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital, and Changping District Hospital on February 19. Among the eight patients, there were 5 men and 3 women; the youngest was 27 years old and the oldest was 69 years old.

From 09:00 to 24:00 on February 19th, two new cases of new coronary pneumonia were confirmed in Beijing, both of whom were close contacts of confirmed cases, and have been sent to designated medical institutions for treatment. There were 25 new suspected cases and 125 existing suspected cases. Sixteen new close contacts were added, and a total of 2091 close contacts were identified, of which 1,419 were released from medical observation.

As of 24:00 on February 19, a total of 395 confirmed cases were diagnosed in Beijing. 153 patients were discharged, 4 died, and 19 were critically ill. 12 cases in Dongcheng District, 53 cases in Xicheng District, 58 cases in Chaoyang District, 61 cases in Haidian District, 40 cases in Fengtai District, 14 cases in Shijingshan District, 3 cases in Mentougou District, 16 cases in Fangshan District, 19 cases in Tongzhou District, and 10 cases in Shunyi District There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, 1 case in Yanqing District, and 26 cases came from overseas. No cases have been reported in Pinggu District.

Of the 395 confirmed cases, 187 were male cases, accounting for 47.3%, and 208 were female cases, accounting for 52.7%; the age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 were under 5 years old, accounting for 3.5%, and 6 to 17 years old. There were 13 cases, accounting for 3.3%, 260 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 65.8%, 108 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 27.4%.
