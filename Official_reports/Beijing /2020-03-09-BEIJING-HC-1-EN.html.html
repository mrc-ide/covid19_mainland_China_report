Yesterday, no new confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia were reported in Beijing, 7 were cured and discharged
Date: 2020-03-09                 Source: Beijing Municipal Health Committee

Between 00:00 and 24:00 on March 8, no new confirmed cases of new coronary pneumonia were reported in Beijing. Eight new suspected cases were reported, and no close contacts were reported. Seven cases of discharged patients were cured and discharged from two designated hospitals in the urban area. There are 4 men and 3 women, the youngest is 37 years old and the oldest is 67 years old.

As of 24:00 on March 8, a total of 428 confirmed cases, 315 cured cases and 8 dead cases were cured. There are 35 suspected cases. A total of 3,632 close contacts have been identified, of which 501 are still in isolation medical observation. 14 confirmed cases in the Middle East, 53 in Xicheng District, 72 in Chaoyang District, 63 in Haidian District, 43 in Fengtai District, 14 in Shijingshan District, 3 in Mentougou District, 16 in Fangshan District, 19 in Tongzhou District, and 10 in Shunyi District There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, and 1 case in Yanqing District. There were no cases in Pinggu District, 25 cases came from outside Beijing, and 13 cases were imported abroad.

There are no new confirmed cases in 12 districts in the city for more than 14 consecutive days, specifically no cases reported in Pinggu District since the outbreak, Yanqing District for 45 days, Mentougou District for 35 days, Huairou District for 31 days, Shunyi District for 29 days, and Miyun District 26 days, 24 days in Shijingshan District, 24 days in Daxing District, 21 days in Fangshan District, 20 days in Changping District, 18 days in Xicheng District, and 18 days in Tongzhou District.

Of the 428 confirmed cases, 205 were male cases, accounting for 47.9%, and 223 were female cases, accounting for 52.1%; the age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 were under 5 years, accounting for 3.3%, and 6 to 17 years There were 14 cases, accounting for 3.3%, 289 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 67.5%, and 111 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 25.9%.
