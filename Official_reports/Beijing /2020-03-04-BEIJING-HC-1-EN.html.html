3 new cases of new coronary pneumonia confirmed in Beijing, 2 of which are imported cases
Date: 2020-03-04                 Source: Beijing Municipal Commission of Health

 

Between March 0 and 24:00 on March 3, 3 new cases of new coronary pneumonia were confirmed in Beijing, of which 2 were imported cases abroad (1 was imported from Iran, 1 was imported from Italy), and 1 was local to our city. All cases have been sent to designated medical institutions for treatment. 11 new suspected cases and 61 close contacts were reported. Six discharged patients were cured and discharged from two designated hospitals in the urban area. There are 3 men and 3 women, the youngest is 18 years old and the oldest is 60 years old.

As of 24:00 on March 3, a total of 417 confirmed cases, 294 cured cases and 8 dead cases were cured. There are 38 suspected cases. A total of 2,796 close contacts have been identified, of which 309 are still in isolation medical observation. 13 confirmed cases in the Middle East, 53 in Xicheng, 71 in Chaoyang, 63 in Haidian, 43 in Fengtai, 14 in Shijingshan District, 3 in Mentougou District, 16 in Fangshan District, 19 in Tongzhou District, and 10 in Shunyi District There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, and 1 case in Yanqing District. There were no cases in Pinggu District, 25 cases came from overseas, and 4 cases came from overseas.

There are no new confirmed cases in 11 districts in the city for more than 14 consecutive days, specifically no reported cases in Pinggu District since the outbreak, Yanqing District for 40 days, Mentougou District for 30 days, Huairou District for 26 days, Shunyi District for 24 days, Dongcheng District 21 days, 21 days in Miyun District, 19 days in Shijingshan District, 19 days in Daxing District, 16 days in Fangshan District, and 15 days in Changping District.

Of the 417 confirmed cases, 202 were male cases, accounting for 48.4%, and 215 were female cases, accounting for 51.6%; the age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 cases were under 5 years, accounting for 3.4%, and 6 to 17 years old There were 13 cases, accounting for 3.1%, 279 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 66.9%, and 111 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 26.6%.
