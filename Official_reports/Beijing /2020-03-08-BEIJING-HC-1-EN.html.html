Two new cases of new coronary pneumonia imported from abroad, five cured and discharged from hospital
Date: 2020-03-08                 Source: Beijing Municipal Commission of Health

 

From 00:00 to 24:00 on March 7, two new cases of new coronary pneumonia were confirmed in Beijing, one from an imported case from Italy and one from Spain, all of which have been sent to designated medical institutions for treatment. 3 new cases were reported and 87 were in close contact. Five patients were cured and discharged from two designated hospitals in the urban area. There are 3 men and 2 women.

As of 24:00 on March 7, a total of 428 confirmed cases, 308 cured cases and 8 dead cases were cured. There are 27 suspected cases. A total of 3,632 close contacts have been identified, of which 551 are still in isolation medical observation. 14 confirmed cases in the Middle East, 53 in Xicheng District, 72 in Chaoyang District, 63 in Haidian District, 43 in Fengtai District, 14 in Shijingshan District, 3 in Mentougou District, 16 in Fangshan District, 19 in Tongzhou District, and 10 in Shunyi District There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, and 1 case in Yanqing District. There were no cases in Pinggu District, 25 cases came from outside Beijing, and 13 cases were imported abroad.

Twelve districts in the city have had no new confirmed cases for more than 14 consecutive days. Specifically, there have been no reported cases in Pinggu District since the outbreak. Yanqing District has 44 days, Mentougou District 34 days, Huairou District 30 days, Shunyi District 28 days, and Miyun District. 25 days, 23 days in Shijingshan District, 23 days in Daxing District, 20 days in Fangshan District, 19 days in Changping District, 17 days in Xicheng District, and 17 days in Tongzhou District.

Of the 428 confirmed cases, 205 were male cases, accounting for 47.9%, and 223 were female cases, accounting for 52.1%; the age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 were under 5 years, accounting for 3.3%, and 6 to 17 years There were 14 cases, accounting for 3.3%, 289 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 67.5%, and 111 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 25.9%.
