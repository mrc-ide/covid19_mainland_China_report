Epidemic situation briefing on the press conference on the prevention and control of new crown pneumonia in Beijing on February 21
Date: 2020-02-21                 Source: Beijing Municipal Health Committee

In Beijing, there were 16 new patients with neonatal pneumonia. After careful treatment by medical staff, they met the discharge standards. On February 20, they were respectively discharged from Ditan Hospital, Youan Hospital, Fengtai Integrated Traditional Chinese and Western Medicine Hospital, Shunyi District Hospital, and Chaoyang District. The community health service center was discharged. Of the 16 patients, there were 10 males and 6 females; the youngest was 6 years old and the oldest was 69 years old.

From 00:00 to 24:00 on February 20, a new confirmed case of new coronary pneumonia was added in Beijing. As a close contact of the confirmed case, it has been sent to designated medical institutions for treatment. There were 41 new suspected cases and 114 existing suspected cases. 4 new close contacts were added, and a total of 2095 close contacts were identified, of which 1488 cases have been released from medical observation.

As of 24:00 on February 20, a total of 396 confirmed cases were diagnosed in Beijing. 169 patients were discharged, 4 died, and 19 were critically ill. 12 cases in Dongcheng District, 53 cases in Xicheng District, 58 cases in Chaoyang District, 61 cases in Haidian District, 41 cases in Fengtai District, 14 cases in Shijingshan District, 3 cases in Mentougou District, 16 cases in Fangshan District, 19 cases in Tongzhou District, and 10 cases in Shunyi District. There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, 1 case in Yanqing District, and 26 cases came from overseas. No cases have been reported in Pinggu District.

Of the 396 confirmed cases, 187 were male cases, accounting for 47.2%, and 209 were female cases, accounting for 52.8%. The age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 were under 5 years old, accounting for 3.5%, and 6 to 17 years old 13 cases, accounting for 3.3%, 260 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 65.7%, 109 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 27.5%.
