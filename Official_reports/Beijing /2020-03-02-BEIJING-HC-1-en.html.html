1 new confirmed case of new coronary pneumonia in Beijing, 6 cured and discharged
Date: 2020-03-02                 Source: Beijing Municipal Commission of Health

Between March 1 and 20:00, a new confirmed case of new coronary pneumonia was added to Beijing. As a close contact of the confirmed case, it has been sent to a designated medical institution for treatment. Twelve new suspected cases and 38 close contacts were reported. Six discharged patients were cured and discharged from two designated hospitals in the urban area. Among them are 1 male and 5 female, the youngest is 3 years old and the oldest is 56 years old.

As of 24:00 on March 1, a total of 414 confirmed cases, 282 cured cases and 8 dead cases were cured. There are 42 suspected cases. A total of 2728 close contacts have been identified, of which 413 are still in isolation medical observation. 13 confirmed cases in the Middle East, 53 in Xicheng, 71 in Chaoyang, 63 in Haidian, 42 in Fengtai, 14 in Shijingshan District, 3 in Mentougou District, 16 in Fangshan District, 19 in Tongzhou District, and 10 in Shunyi District There were 29 cases in Changping District, 39 cases in Daxing District, 7 cases in Huairou District, 7 cases in Miyun District, 1 case in Yanqing District, 25 cases came from overseas, and 2 cases came from overseas. No cases have been reported in Pinggu District.

There are no new confirmed cases in 10 districts in the city for more than 14 consecutive days, specifically no cases reported in Pinggu District since the outbreak, 38 days in Yanqing District, 28 days in Mentougou District, 24 days in Huairou District, 22 days in Shunyi District, and Dongcheng District 19 days, 19 days in Miyun District, 17 days in Shijingshan District, 17 days in Daxing District, and 14 days in Fangshan District.

Among the 414 confirmed cases, 200 were male cases, accounting for 48.3%, and 214 were female cases, accounting for 51.7%; the age range was 6 months to 94 years, of which 14 cases were under 5 years, accounting for 3.4%, and 6 to 17 years old There were 13 cases, accounting for 3.1%, 277 cases aged 18 to 59, accounting for 66.9%, and 110 cases aged 60 and above, accounting for 26.6%.
