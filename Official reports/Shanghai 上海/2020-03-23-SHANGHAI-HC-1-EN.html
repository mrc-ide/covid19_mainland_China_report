No new confirmed cases of local neo-coronary pneumonia on March 22 in Shanghai
/

/ ( 2020-03-23)/

/
/
/ /

3At 04:00 on the 22nd of the month, 10 confirmed cases of imported neocoronary pneumonia were reported through the port joint prevention and control mechanism. As of 24:00 on March 22, a total of 66 overseas imported confirmed cases were reported, and there are currently 18 suspected overseas imported cases under investigation.

Case 1 is from Anhui, China, travelling in Burkina Faso, departing from Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso on March 17 and arriving at Shanghai Pudong International on March 18 after transiting through Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. At the airport, they will be observed in isolation after entering the customs. Symptoms will appear during this period. Based on comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical symptoms, laboratory tests, and imaging findings, the diagnosis is confirmed.

Case 2 was of Spanish nationality. He departed from Dubai, UAE on March 19 and arrived at Shanghai Hongqiao Airport on the same day after transiting through Beijing Capital International Airport. After entering the country, he was isolated and observed with symptoms. Based on comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical symptoms, laboratory tests, and imaging findings, the diagnosis is confirmed.

Case 3 is of British nationality. It departed from London, UK on March 19, and arrived at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 20 after transiting through Bangkok, Thailand. After entering the country, it was isolated and observed. It integrated epidemiological history, clinical manifestations, and laboratory The results of tests and imaging examinations were diagnosed as confirmed cases.

Case 4 is a U.S. citizen, departing from New York, USA on March 19, and arriving at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 21 after transiting through Tokyo, Japan. After entering the country, she was isolated for observation, and integrated epidemiological history, clinical manifestations, and laboratory The results of tests and imaging examinations were diagnosed as confirmed cases.

Case 5 was from Henan, China, studying in the United Kingdom, departing from London, UK on March 15, and arriving at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 16 after transiting through Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. She was isolated for observation after entering customs, and symptoms occurred during the period. Based on comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical symptoms, laboratory tests, and imaging findings, the diagnosis is confirmed.

Case 6 is from Shanghai, China, studying in the United Kingdom, departing from London, England on March 16, and arriving at Shanghai Hongqiao Airport on March 17, after transiting in Guangzhou. Isolation and observation will be conducted after entry. Comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical performance, The results of laboratory tests and imaging tests were diagnosed as confirmed cases.

Cases 7 and 8 are mother and daughter, of French nationality. They departed from Paris, France on March 20, and arrived at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 21. They were isolated and observed after entering customs. They combined epidemiological history, clinical manifestations, and experiments. Laboratory tests and imaging findings were diagnosed as confirmed cases.

Case 9 is from Shanghai, China, visiting relatives in the United States, departing from New York, United States on March 16, and arriving at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 17 after transiting through Taipei. She was isolated and observed after entering customs, and symptoms occurred during the period. Based on comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical symptoms, laboratory tests, and imaging findings, the diagnosis is confirmed.

Case 10 is a Chinese national from Shanghai, studying in the United States, departing from Chicago, the United States on March 18, and arriving at Shanghai Pudong International Airport on March 19 after transiting through Taipei. She was isolated for observation after entering the customs, and symptoms occurred during the period. Based on comprehensive epidemiological history, clinical symptoms, laboratory tests, and imaging findings, the diagnosis is confirmed.

3At 04:00 on the 22nd, there were no new confirmed cases of local new coronary pneumonia. As of 24:00 on March 22, a total of 338 locally confirmed cases were reported, and one suspected local case is currently under investigation.

3At 04:00 on the 22nd, one case was cured and discharged. As of 12:00 on March 24, a total of 328 patients had been discharged and 4 died. There are 72 cases in the hospital (including 65 imported cases), of which 62 cases are stable, 3 cases are severe, 7 cases are critical.
